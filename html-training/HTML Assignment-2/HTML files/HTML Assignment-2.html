  • <table>: Defines the table
  • <thead>: Defines the table header section
  • <tbody>: Defines the table body section
  • <tr>: Defines a table row
  • <th>: Defines a header cell in a table
  • <td>: Defines a standard cell in a table

This webpage demonstrates the use of named anchors in HTML. Named anchors help users navigate within the same webpage. They are especially useful when the webpage contains large content. Instead of scrolling manually, users can jump to sections directly. This improves usability and user experience. Named anchors are commonly used in documentation pages. They are also useful in FAQs and long articles. In this example, department sections are linked. Each section contains detailed information. Clicking a link scrolls the page automatically.

The following sections describe different engineering departments. Each department is represented using a named anchor. The anchor is linked using the href attribute. Classes are used only for styling and grouping. Navigation does not work using class alone. The name attribute enables scrolling behavior. Enough content is added for visible scrolling. This makes the demonstration clear and effective. Such examples are commonly asked in practical exams. This format is ideal for lab records and viva.

Computer Science Engineering (CSE)

The Computer Science Engineering department focuses on computing technologies. It covers subjects such as programming, data structures, and algorithms. Students learn problem solving and logical thinking skills. Programming languages like C, C++, Java, and Python are taught. The department also includes subjects like operating systems. Database management systems are a core part of the curriculum. Web development and software engineering are also emphasized. Modern topics include artificial intelligence and machine learning. Students work on real-world projects and applications. This prepares them for careers in the IT industry.

The CSE department offers excellent career opportunities. Graduates can work as software engineers and developers. Many students choose roles in data science and analytics. Some pursue higher education and research. The department encourages innovation and creativity. Hackathons and coding contests are regularly conducted. Industry internships provide practical exposure. Students gain both theoretical and practical knowledge. Teamwork and communication skills are also developed. This makes CSE one of the most popular engineering branches.

Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE)

The Electronics and Communication Engineering department focuses on electronics. It includes the study of circuits and electronic devices. Communication systems form a major part of the syllabus. Students learn about analog and digital communication. Signal processing is another important subject. The department also covers embedded systems. Microcontrollers and microprocessors are taught in detail. Students gain knowledge of VLSI design. IoT and wireless communication are emerging areas. Practical lab work supports theoretical learning.

ECE graduates have diverse career opportunities. They can work in electronics and telecom industries. Many jobs are available in embedded systems companies. Students can work in semiconductor industries. Some graduates enter government and PSU sectors. Research and higher studies are also popular choices. The department focuses on hands-on learning. Industrial visits are regularly organized. Technical workshops enhance practical skills. ECE plays a crucial role in modern technology.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering is one of the oldest engineering branches. It focuses on design and manufacturing of mechanical systems. Core subjects include thermodynamics and fluid mechanics. Manufacturing processes are studied in detail. Students learn about machines and mechanical design. Strength of materials is an important subject. Robotics and automation are modern additions. CAD and CAM tools are widely used. Practical workshops form a major part of learning. The branch is essential for industrial development.

Mechanical engineers work in various industries. They are employed in automotive and aerospace sectors. Power plants and manufacturing industries hire graduates. Many work in design and production roles. Some engineers choose careers in research. Higher studies are also a common option. Mechanical engineers contribute to infrastructure development. Innovation and problem solving are key skills. The branch offers stable and diverse career paths. Mechanical engineering remains highly relevant today.
